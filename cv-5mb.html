About

I focus on the intersection of AI safety and computational neuroscience, where my research centers on developing methods to analyze the relationship between speech and neural data. By aligning neural network embeddings with time-resolved brain activity, I aim to deepen our understanding of how the brain processes auditory and language information.

Building on my background in computational neuroscience, I now focus on AI safety—specifically, developing model evaluations and Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF). I’m driven by the challenge of aligning AI systems with human values and user needs. In my view, developing techniques to elicit preferences from both models and users is essential for the secure deployment of AI and for optimizing its performance in real-world applications.

For more details on my research approach, see my blog post: Reverse Evaluations: Modeling Reward Functions from User Interaction.

My goal is to develop reliable AI systems that are aligned with human values and users' long-term objectives, while fostering scientific progress. I see these goals as mutually reinforcing.
